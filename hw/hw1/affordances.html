Real Affordance Perceived Affordance

Affordance is any possible action of a user upon an object and may not be visible, known, or desired.


Can by limited by various constraints, depending on the variables.


The perceivable aspect of affordances are known as signifiers.

Perceived affordance is any action the user perceives as possible.


As a designer, what the user perceives is more important that what is true.
Example Example

With a file folder, possible actions include picking it up, opening it, storing other items inside, retrieving previously stored items, labelling the folder, etc.

With a digital icon, the perceived (not literal) actions may include clicking to see stored contents or edit the name, right-clicking to access more options, click and drag to move the folder around.
